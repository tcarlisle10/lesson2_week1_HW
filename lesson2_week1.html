The USS Texas

The USS Texas is a historic battleship that served in both World War I and World War II. Commissioned in 1914, it is known for being the first U.S. battleship to launch an aircraft and the first to have anti-aircraft guns. The Texas participated in key operations, including the D-Day invasion at Normandy and the battles at Iwo Jima and Okinawa. After its active service, it was decommissioned in 1948 and became a museum ship in Texas. Today, the USS Texas is a National Historic Landmark, representing a significant piece of naval history.
